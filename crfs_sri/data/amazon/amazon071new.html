By John B. GoodeTOP 500 REVIEWER
Size Name:300 Liquid Soft Gels|Item Package Quantity:1
In my quest for heart health I'm not sticking with one brand but trying all different types of fish oil or cod liver oil. This purchase is my latest one.

1) Cost: It appears that the different people have bought bottles of different potency, so my number are only applicable to those who have bought bottles that have 300mg per capsule.

The cost per oz. or cost per capsule is often misleading because different products (even within brands) have different potency. To find the real cost, divide the cost by the total amount of DHA+EPA (the key omega-3's) in a bottle.

For example, my bottle has 150 servings (300 capsules) at 600mg DHA+EPA per serving (2 capsules per serving). That's 150 servings X 600mg DHA+EPA = 90,000mg or 90g of DHA+EPA per bottle. Divide current cost by 90g will give you the price per gram. And to that you have to add sales tax if applicable and shipping. It's usually more cost effective to buy enough items to get free shipping from Amazon.

2) The product itself: The capsules are big, bigger than other fish oil capsules I'm used to. I also like to bite the capsule as I don't like swallowing pills of any sort. As this fish oil is from sardines and anchovies I can tell you that they have a very strong anchovy taste, if you can't stand anchovies on your pizza you might not like this, but I'm fine. I usually have this with tea or coffee so any fish taste is instantly washed away. And some other products have lemon oil to disguise the taste. For me, I like sushi, so fishy taste doesn't bother me at all. (After taking about 100 gels I don't notice the taste anymore.)

3) Safety: This product specifically states that dioxins, PCBs, furans, and mercury are removed or not detectable within certain limits. I will only buy products that has something like that on it. For example, I was going to buy the NatureMade cod liver oil, but I could not find that statement anywhere on it's website or on the bottle and so I passed on that and got this instead.

4) Potency: About 1000 mg of fish oil is recommended per day from most sources that I read; the FDA does not give a recommendation. Every product is a little different but they seem to range +/- 200mg of that recommendation. This product gives you 2400mg fish oil per day in two capsules. Some products give you their recommended dosage all the way from 1 to 5 capsules, i.e. you take from 1 to 5 capsules to get that recommended dosage. 5 capsules seem a lot but one of the Carlson products does exactly that.

Summary:
Source: Small fish (anchovy and sardines), deep ocean, not fish farmed.
Method: Molecular distillation.
Toxins: Mercury, PCB's, dioxins removed/not detected.
Amounts:
1200mg total fish oil per capsule.
EPA+DHA 300mg per capsule.
EPA+DHA 90,000mg/90g per bottle (300 capsules).

IFOS: The International Fish Oil Standards Program ("IFOS") (Google "IFOS consumer reports") is an independent third party omega-3 testing organization that provides batch-specific summaries of a product's IFOS testing results. The reports include a 5-star rating for each product containing greater than 60% concentration of EPA and DHA per gram of fish oil, allowing consumers to compare fish oil products based on their safety, purity and quality. Most of the highly rated omega-3 fish oil products are listed. Nature Made Fish Oil is not listed or rated.

SOY CONTENT: Nutrigold Triple Strength Fish Oil Omega-3 Gold, OmegaVia Pharma-Grade Fish Oil, Norwegian Gold Ultimate Fish Oils Super Critical Omega, Nordic Naturals Ultimate Omega, and Omapure Pharmaceutical Grade Omega-3 Fish Oil, do not contain soy. Garden of Life Minami Nutrition Platinum Omega 3 Plus D3 Formula, Now Ultra Omega-3, and Nature Made Fish Oil contain soy.

GMO CONTENT: None of these supplements contain GMOs.

ENTERIC COATING: OmegaVia Pharma-Grade Fish Oil, Norwegian Gold Ultimate Fish Oils Super Critical Omega, and Now Ultra Omega-3 are three of the few omega-3 fish oil products that are enteric coated. Enteric coating is used to prevent fish oil capsules from being digested in the stomach, which can cause a "fishy reflux." However, this problem is limited to only low grade, poorly manufactured fish oil products. Highly purified fish oil supplements that are manufactured to maintain freshness do not generally produce this fishy reflux, especially if the product is taken with food as recommended. Further, the only people who experience fishy burps from even high quality fish oil supplements are those with sensitive GI tract issues, for whom enteric coating may provide some relief from fishy burps, but may also interfere with absorption of the key nutrients in the supplement.

There is some evidence that suggests that enteric coating is not required and may actually hinder or impede proper absorption of omega-3 fish oil. In my view, however, this body of evidence is not conclusive.

Bottom line, if you have sensitive GI tract issues and experience fishy burps from omega-3 fish oil supplements, you may want to consider an enteric coated product.

SUPERCRITICAL DUAL-PROCESS CO2 TECHNOLOGY: Garden of Life Minami Nutrition Platinum Omega 3 Plus D3 Formula and Norwegian Gold Ultimate Fish Oils Super Critical Omega are not molecularly distilled. They use a proprietary Supercritical Dual-Process CO2 technology which concentrates omega-3s, yielding unique selectivity of one of the most biologically active omega-3 fatty acids - EPA. Using less heat, less repeat processing, and natural carbon dioxide as a solvent, allows these products to reach peak concentrations approximating 100% EPA, omitting superfluous fats and fillers. 400% less heat than molecular distillation is used during the entire process, with no oxygen exposure. Natural CO2 is used as an alternative to organic solvents and protects the final product from oxidation. In my opinion, Supercritical Dual-Process CO2 technology is superior to molecular distillation.

VITAMIN D3: Norwegian Gold Ultimate Fish Oils Super Critical Omega contains 1000 I.U. of Vitamin D3. Garden of Life Minami Nutrition Platinum Omega 3 Plus D3 Formula contains 300 I.U. of Vitamin D3. I don't believe this is terribly significant, as Vitamin D3 can be purchased for very little money, and lots of folks who take it consume more than 1000 I.U. daily.

LIPASE: Norwegian Gold Ultimate Fish Oils Super Critical Omega contains 4 mgs of Lipase, which is a fat digesting enzyme. Most omega-3 fish oil products do not contain Lipase. However, the human body produces more than adequate amounts of Lipase and, therefore, Norwegian Gold Ultimate Fish Oils Super Critical Omega reference to its benefits might be somewhat enthusiastic.

MEALS: Any ethyl esters based and triglycerides based omega-3 fish oils are best taken with a meal containing some fat, as they are more efficiently absorbed when taken with food.

OPINION: There are very few things I like about Nature Made Fish Oil. It is very cheap; it contains no GMOs; and it contains no mercury. On the other hand, it is not IFOS five star rated; it has an extremely low omega-3 fish oil potency (30%); it contains soy; it is not certified Kosher; and it is not vegetarian.

I'm not impressed with Nature Made Fish Oil despite its low cost. I question its effectiveness considering its extremely low omega-3 fish oil potency. I put it on a par with Kirkland Natural Fish Oil Omega-3 Concentrate, another cheap omega-3 fish oil product. Common sense tells me that any omega-3 pill that costs $.05 per gel cap is not very good! You can get it at Costco in a 400 gel cap bottle for about $.03 per gel cap. In this case, you get what you pay for.

Ironically, Nature Made Fish Oil is currently the No. 1 ranked omega-3 fish oil supplement sold on Amazon.
