5.0 out of 5 starsOnline backup is free!May 18, 2013
By Doc
Amazon Verified Purchase
I needed a reliable, cost effective security camera for basic home needs. I compared this product to Dropcam, Foscam, Logitech indoor master, etc. I ended up choosing the y-cam home monitor indoor because it was the only one that provides free online backup for up to 7 days at a time. All the others require a monthly or yearly fee. This camera's picture is very good, the IR for nighttime is very good, but you can't use it through glass (like all other indoor IR cameras). I ended up putting a Mr Beams MB390 300-Lumen Weatherproof Wireless Battery Powered LED Ultra Bright Spotlight with Motion Sensor, Brown outside my house. This provides enough light to make the IR detect anyone through the glass at night. I also used the Panavise 809 Camera Window Suction-Cup Mount with Panavise 812-03 3-Inch Extension for 809 Suction Cup Mount. This allowed me to mount my security camera to my window very securely. This camera is wireless so no ethernet cable needed (other than initial setup) and uses outlet adapter for power supply.

The setup was easy. Be sure you buy the HOME MONITOR y-cam (new 2013) and not the other products that they sell separately. I use an Apple Time Capsule wireless router. The iPhone app is nice. I can click a button for live view, turn off the camera, turn off motion activation, or turn off alerts. It also has a nice "archive" button that will let you view all of your motion activated recordings. The iPad app has same functions and is nice. My wife can also access the same app on her phone. The online website is secure and allows you to setup a field of view for motion activation. You can create 2 zones for motion activation and you can adjust the sensitivity for the activation for both zones. Very nice! You can also save your film clips to your computer from the website. We have both a mac and pc and the website works well with both.

Since the camera must send the video online to archive and then sends you an email there is a delay in accessing your motion activated content from the given moment it happens. This can vary from 30 seconds to 1 minute. However, you can click on live view. There is still a delay as the camera must send the information to your device through the Internet. I found out that the real time delay for my iPhone was about 30 seconds and iPad real time delay was about 25 seconds. Both of my apple devices are connected to my wifi network. For my computer, it was only a 7 second delay with the real time video from what was currently going on and what I was able to see clicking on the live button.

Likes: Cheap. Free online backup. It only sends video via wireless to the online backup when the motion activation was triggered. This can save you a lot of memory if you have Internet activity limitations. Very good picture. IR is nice when not next to a window but putting having outside light can help. I did email customer service (they responded in one day with a very nice and lengthy email) and they said that they may release a patch so that you can turn off the IR if needed. Wireless is nice. It also is light.

Cons: Realtime delay, but can be minimized by using pc to activate live feed. Frames-per-second is low, but average. Microphone is good, but not 2 way. Wish it could send text messages or have 2 emails to send alerts to.

Conclusion: There are several positive reviews with actual footage that you can find on youtube and by searching Internet. Consider this excellent, cost-effective option. Read more

5.0 out of 5 starsAlways a lot of research before purchaseAugust 4, 2013
By Wave Crasher
Amazon Verified Purchase
After a lot of research, I purchased two HomeMonitor systems. The setup is easy...as long as you read the instructions. The fit and finish is solid. I ordered them on this last Wednesday using the two day delivery and they arrived on Friday. I didn't get both working until Saturday simply because the placement of one of the cameras had a VERY weak wifi signal. A trip to buy a wifi extender solved that issue and it has been working flawlessly ever since.

The video is better than expected and the 7 day cloud storage is a deal maker. The iPhone app could use some more functions, such as remembering the password (says it does, but not) and the ability to erase separate clips.

I would recommend this to a friend with the following advice. First make sure you have good wifi signal strength in the location you pick for the camera. Secondly, most of us have more than one email account and I suggest using the least used for your notification account. Otherwise, until you get comfortable with when to toggle on & off and have your motion sensors correct, you will find your IN-BOX filling up quickly with notifications.

3.0 out of 5 starsThe good and the badJuly 16, 2013
By travelguy73
Good: picture quality is pretty good, and night vision is surprisingly strong when objects aren't too far away. Small, unobtrusive design. Nice web interface and the email alerts worked as expected.

Bad: Had a lot of trouble getting it to work with my Linksys router (for Wi-Fi, wired access worked well). The motion sensor is where I have a real issue: It doesn't come to life fast enough for me. I tested it by placing it on my kitchen counter, pointed at the back door. I then went to the office and let it run all day, looking to see when I would get emails that movement had been detected. It emailed me promptly when it detected movement, however, when I would watch the replay online, I would *maybe* get the trail end of the person, but usually I wouldn't get any action at all, because by the time the camera sprung to life, the person had already walked by. That's not good, IMO.

I can see it working in some circumstances, but I ended up returning the product because it isn't what I'm looking for.
