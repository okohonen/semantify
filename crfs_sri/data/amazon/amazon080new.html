5.0 out of 5 starsNot perfect, but close enoughApril 30, 2013
By Ayazaya
Amazon Verified Purchase
I started college with a backpack my mom got for me from costco (ful I think) which I found very cumbersome with regards to the height (especially when biking which is my only means of transportation aside from walking), shoulder straps, and the sheer ridiculous amount of pockets it had. I started looking for a new backpack with the following in mind: it had to comfortable size wise (so shorter than 19"), had to have 4 or fewer pockets, no computer/tablet slot, and be sturdy enough to withstand the constant strenous strain of college. Overall, I freaking love this thing. I didn't think I'd ever hold this much affection for an inanimate object as I do for my backpack. I normally never write any reviews since I don't usually find the product important/satisfying enough to bother with one. But I have to say this backpack deserves it!

Pros:
Color: I bought this in the ash/chartreuse and I must say I love the color combination! It's just enough green to make it stylish, but not gaudy; and I found the grey to be more feminine than the standard black fare.

Size: The size is deceptively small. On the outside it looks quite svelte, but you can fit a lot stuff without too much trouble. I generally carry 4-5 notebooks, 1 1" binder, 2-3 smallish paperbacks, 10" tablet w/case, and if necessary during midterm/final's week my ochem textbook which is a bit thicker than a 2" binder, and a pen case along with other knickknacks like my phone, water bottle (1L so bigger than most), etc. It fit everything nicely and without any problems!

Construction: I have to hand it to High Sierra, they made this backpack quite resilient. I've had my backpack since October of 2012 and have stuffed it with books and textbooks until I thought I would break my back and still it withstood it all quite well. There has been no fraying around where the straps connect and I've had no problems with the zipper, even when I stuffed it. I'm not sure if it's waterproof or not, but when I was drenched, my notebooks and thankfully my notes were all dry.

Cons:

I find that with a heavy load, the straps get rather uncomfortable and with extended wear start digging into your shoulders (heavy load as in 2-3 textbooks and 4-5 small hardback books).

There is only ONE! side-pocket. This is a bit annoying since I generally take my water bottle and coffee cup/thermos thing with me; I have to put my waterbottle inside the pack which reduces the amount of total stuff I can carry around and messes up the weight distribution. Moreover, the mesh of the side pocket tore after a month and I had to mend it by sewing a protective patch around the mesh.

It doesn't have a body of it's own so it slouches if you take a lot of stuff out. It might not be a big deal to most, but it drives me insane when it slouches and falls around while in a cramped lecture hall since it makes taking things in and out a bit trickier. Of course this was remedied with the binder, but otherwise it doesn't stand on its own. Read more ›

4.0 out of 5 starsUsing to carry a bowling ball...making an internal frame possibly to carry glass as well (where I'm coming from)August 12, 2013
By E. Ament
Amazon Verified Purchase
The backpack I was using to carry a bowling ball in a box (the box works like an internal frame) zipper broke on me when I tried to put shoes too like an idiot. I was just looking for something cheap and had a decent amount of space. It has much different material compared to the JanSport my parents bought me in the 90's.

Pros
-Good material. Very stiff and seems impossible to tear. I imagine if you try and carry it by one shoulder strap that physics will cause that to tear though.

Cons
-Zipper is much shorter in main compartment than my old one. It also could be closer to the top point of the bag. It's not really something I noticed before buying, but I've been looking at other ones and most have short zippers now to probably put less strain on the zipper when filling it full. Under Armour looks like they have longer zippers, but cost more too. It was hard to put the box in for an internal frame which is needed to keep the ball from digging in your back. I did though and that's why I'm keeping it.
-My old JanSport expanded better I think due to the polyester that is used by them compared to the High Sierra which uses cross woven nylon. As a result, my box didn't fit as well even though this bag is actually deeper I think (by one inch if I'm right). Space is sacrificed for toughness.

Anyways, I hope this helps some people that might want to carry a bowling ball or put an internal frame in a cheap bag to carry glass or something.
